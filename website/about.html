About Rory Roberts

20 years of identifying risks at the world's largest financial institutions.

The Story

In 2016, I was appointed Global Head of Risk Identification at Credit Suisse. I built a team of seven people across London, Zurich, and India, and we were responsible for identifying every material risk facing the bank. The first thing I discovered was that there was no standard methodology anywhere. Not at Credit Suisse, not at any bank. Every institution was reinventing the wheel — building ad hoc processes from scratch, with no common framework, no shared vocabulary, and no way to benchmark whether what they were doing was any good.

Before Credit Suisse, I spent almost a decade at Zurich Insurance. The first five years were as Solvency II Project Manager, implementing the regulatory framework across the group. After that, I moved to the front office as a dealer — trading FX, money markets, derivatives, and options. Before Zurich, I was a Quant Risk Analyst at AIB, where I led the Basel II derivatives pricing team. And before that, I was a Financial Risk Manager at Hypo Real Estate, pricing CDOs and mortgage-backed securities in the run-up to the financial crisis.

In 2023, I took on the role of Risk Identification Lead at Standard Chartered, working within the Enterprise Risk Management department. The focus was integrating risk identification directly into the bank's ICAAP and ILAAP processes — making sure that the risks feeding into capital and liquidity adequacy assessments were identified through a rigorous, documented methodology rather than inherited from last year's register.

I founded EON Risk Services in 2020 because I believed this methodology should be available to every bank, not just the ones that can afford to hire a Global Head of Risk Identification. The process I built at Credit Suisse, refined at Standard Chartered, and grounded in the regulatory frameworks I implemented at Zurich Insurance — that process shouldn't live in my head or in a single institution. It should be a standard that any bank, of any size, can adopt and defend to their regulator.

My background is in mathematical physics — I hold a B.Sc from University College Dublin (1994–1998). I'm a certified Financial Risk Manager (GARP) and hold the ACI Dealing Certificate. I started my career writing COBOL in Melbourne, then moved to building VMS/VAX financial analytics systems in Pascal and C. Somewhere in between, I taught English in Taiwan and cycled 5,000 kilometres from Bangkok to Xi'an. The thread through all of it has been a fascination with how complex systems fail — and how to build processes that catch the failures before they happen.

## IRISH HOUSING STOCK
- ~2 million homes in Ireland
- 700,000 off-gas-grid homes heated by oil (mostly rural)
- ~500,000 homes heated by gas (mostly urban)
- Common types: semi-detached (most common), detached, terraced, bungalow, apartment
- Pre-2000 homes typically poorly insulated (BER D-G)
- Post-2011 homes built to higher standards (BER A-B)
- Average BER rating nationally: D1-D2
- Solid wall construction common pre-1970s (no cavity)
- Cavity wall construction standard from 1970s onward

## BER (Building Energy Rating) EXPLAINED
- Scale: A1 (best) to G (worst)
- A1-A3: Nearly zero energy, new builds, deep retrofits
- B1-B3: Good — well insulated, efficient heating
- C1-C3: Average modern standard
- D1-D2: National average — significant improvement possible
- E1-G: Poor — high energy waste, urgent upgrade recommended
- BER cert costs ~€150-€200 (€50 SEAI grant available)
- BER required for selling or renting a property
- Improving from D to B can cut energy bills by 40-60%

## SEAI GRANTS (as of March 2026)

### Individual Energy Upgrade Grants (Better Energy Homes)
No income test. Available to all homeowners. Home must be built before 2011.

Insulation:
- Attic insulation: €2,000 grant for detached (typical cost €1,500-€3,000)
- Cavity wall insulation: €1,800 grant (typical cost €1,500-€2,500)
- External wall insulation (detached house): €8,000 grant (typical cost €12,000-€18,000)
- External wall insulation (semi-detached/terrace): €6,000 grant (typical cost €8,000-€14,000)
- Internal dry lining: €3,500 grant (typical cost €5,000-€10,000)
- First-Time Buyers of existing homes: 100% median cost of attic insulation covered
- "Second Wall" measure: homeowners who previously got wall insulation can now apply for another wall type

Heating:
- Heat pump system (air-to-water): up to €12,500 total grant, broken down as:
  - Base heat pump grant: €6,500
  - Heating system upgrade grant: €2,000 (radiators, pipework, cylinders)
  - Renewable Heat Bonus: €4,000 (for removing fossil fuel or electric heating system)
- Apartment heat pump: up to €9,500
- Air-to-air heat pump: up to €7,500
- Heating controls upgrade: €700 grant
- Solar water heating: €1,200 grant (typical cost €3,000-€5,000)

Renewable Energy:
- Solar PV panels: SEAI grant up to €1,800. ZERO VAT on supply and installation
- Battery storage: additional grant available with solar PV

Windows and Doors (NEW from March 2, 2026):
- Detached home: up to €4,000
- Semi-detached / end of terrace: up to €3,000
- Mid-terrace: up to €1,800
- Apartment: up to €1,500
- External doors: €800 per door (max 2 doors, €1,600 total)
- Requirement: home must have good attic/wall insulation already (HLI of 2.3 W/K·m² or better)
- New windows must achieve U-value of 1.4 W/m²K or better

Other:
- BER assessment: €50 grant
- Air tightness testing: included in some packages

Maximum combined grants: Up to approximately €25,000+ for a full deep retrofit.

### One Stop Shop (Full Retrofit)
- For homeowners who want to do multiple upgrades at once
- SEAI-registered One Stop Shop manages the whole project
- Higher grant rates than individual route
- Must achieve minimum BER B2 after works
- Typical cost for full deep retrofit: €40,000-€60,000
- Typical grant: €20,000-€30,000

### Warmer Homes Scheme (Free Upgrades)
- Completely FREE energy upgrades
- For homeowners on certain social welfare payments:
  - Fuel Allowance
  - Working Family Payment
  - One-Parent Family Payment
  - Domiciliary Care Allowance
  - Carer's Allowance (and living with the person being cared for)
  - Disability Allowance (for 6+ months)
  - Job Seekers Allowance (for 6+ months, with children under 7)
- Home must be built before 2006
- Includes: attic insulation, cavity wall insulation, lagging jacket, draught proofing, energy-efficient lighting, heating upgrade if needed
- Wait time: 22-26 months total (pre-works BER ~12 months, survey ~14 months, works complete ~22-26 months)
- NEW March 2026: people on the waiting list can now access individual grants for attic/cavity wall insulation WITHOUT losing their place in the queue

### How to Apply
1. Check eligibility on seai.ie
2. Get a BER assessment first (recommended)
3. Get quotes from SEAI-registered contractors
4. Apply online at seai.ie BEFORE starting any work
5. SEAI approves the application
6. Work is completed by registered contractor
7. Submit proof of payment
8. Grant is paid to homeowner
IMPORTANT: Work must NOT begin before SEAI approval or the grant is void.

## HEAT PUMP ECONOMICS
- Air-to-water heat pump: most common type in Ireland
- COP (Coefficient of Performance): 3.1 typical (produces 3.1 units of heat per 1 unit of electricity = 310% effective efficiency)
- Installation cost: €8,000-€14,000 (before grants)
- SEAI grants: €6,500 (heat pump) + €2,000 (system upgrade) + €4,000 (renewable bonus) = up to €12,500
- Net cost after grants: as low as €1,500-€3,500
- VAT reduced from 23% to 9% on heat pumps (since Jan 2025)

Running cost comparison (3-bed semi, 14,000 kWh annual heat demand):
- Oil boiler (80% efficiency): ~€2,730/year at current kerosene prices (€1.56/L)
- Heat pump on standard electricity (30c/kWh): ~€1,355/year
- Heat pump on night rate (16.34c/kWh): ~€738/year
- Savings vs oil: €1,375-€1,992/year depending on tariff

- Payback period: 6-8 years in well-insulated home (factoring €12,500 grant)
- Best suited for: well-insulated homes (BER C or better), underfloor heating or oversized radiators
- Heat pumps output water at 35-45°C (vs 65-75°C for oil boilers) — need low-temp distribution
- May need radiator upgrades in older homes
- Installing in poorly insulated home = compressor runs constantly, COP degrades, bills spiral
- Lifespan: 15-20 years

## HVO (Hydrotreated Vegetable Oil) — QUICK WIN FOR OIL HOMES
- Drop-in replacement for kerosene in most existing oil boilers
- Made from waste cooking oils and fats via hydrotreatment process
- 88-90% carbon reduction vs kerosene
- Grant and Worcester-Bosch (dominant Irish brands) have certified modern condensing boilers (post-2018) as HVO-compatible
- Conversion for older boilers: replacement nozzle, flame sensor, fuel pump seal — typically done during annual service
- Conversion cost: ~€500 (fraction of heat pump cost)
- Price: slight premium over kerosene but gap narrowing as carbon tax increases
- Major supplier: Certa (DCC Energy) — 22 domestic heating depots nationwide
- Also available: Circle K (30+ forecourts with HVO)
- LIMITATION: HVO supply is finite — can only cover 1-4% of national heat demand
- Treated by policymakers as interim bridge technology, NOT a long-term solution
- Good for: heritage buildings, solid stone walls, or homes where heat pump isn't feasible

## ELECTRICITY TARIFFS & SMART METERS
- 1.9+ million smart meters installed in Ireland
- Smart meters enable time-of-use tariffs — cheaper electricity at off-peak times

### Current Electricity Costs (March 2026)
- National average unit rate: 36.34c/kWh
- Cheapest standard unit rate: 28.80c/kWh (Community Power)
- Cheapest discounted annual bill: €1,390.84 (Ecopower)
- Electric Ireland standard variable: 28.49c/kWh (~€1,427/year)
- Standing charges: €131.69 to €244.77/year depending on supplier and tariff type
- Gas discounted rate: 8.09c/kWh (Flogas dual-fuel)
- Gas standing charge: ~€151/year
- 9% VAT on electricity and gas (extended to end of 2030)
- Switching supplier can save €323-€664/year (use bonkers.ie, switcher.ie, PowerToSwitch.ie)

### Time-of-Use Tariff Structure
- Day rate: ~31.10c/kWh (8am-5pm and 7pm-11pm)
- Peak rate: ~33.18c/kWh (5pm-7pm weekdays)
- Night rate: ~16.34c/kWh (11pm-8am)
- Free electricity hours: some suppliers offer free weekend hours
- Optimized load shifting can save up to €414/year

### Dynamic Electricity Tariffs (DETs) — COMING JUNE 2026
- Mandatory for all suppliers with 200,000+ customers from June 1, 2026
- Prices will fluctuate hourly based on wholesale market
- Suppliers must give 24-hour advance pricing notifications
- Potential savings: 15-20% baseline, up to 38% with solar + battery + automation
- Affected suppliers: Bord Gáis, Electric Ireland, Energia, PrePay Power, SSE Airtricity, Yuno Energy

### How to Save with Smart Meters
- Run washing machine, dryer, dishwasher at night (timer) — night rate is ~half day rate
- Charge EVs overnight
- Heat water overnight with immersion timer
- Avoid heavy electricity use 5pm-7pm weekdays (peak)
- Check if your supplier offers free hours and schedule around them
- Switch supplier annually — loyalty penalty is real (rollover to inflated standard rates)

## SOLAR PV ECONOMICS
- Typical domestic system: 3-6 kWp (8-14 panels)
- Cost: €6,000-€16,000 before grants (depending on size and battery)
- SEAI grant: up to €1,800
- ZERO VAT on supply and installation of domestic solar panels (permanent)
- Battery storage: €3,000-€5,000 additional

Clean Export Guarantee (CEG) — sell excess to grid:
- SSE Airtricity: 32c/kWh (Year 1), 27c/kWh (Year 2) — best rate
- Pinergy: 25c/kWh
- Electric Ireland: 19.5c/kWh
- Bord Gáis / Energia: 18.5c/kWh
- PrePay Power / Yuno: 15.89c/kWh
- First €400 of CEG income is TAX FREE (until 2028)
- Strategy: sell at 32c daytime, buy back at 16c night rate = energy arbitrage

Payback periods (2026 data):
- 3.5 kWp (8 panels): ~8 years 3 months
- 4.4 kWp (10 panels): ~7 years 2 months
- 5.3 kWp (12 panels): ~6 years 5 months
- 6.2 kWp (14 panels): ~5 years 8 months
- Bigger system = shorter payback (economies of scale in installation)

- Best orientation: south-facing roof, 30-40 degree pitch (east/west also viable)
- Panels last 25-30 years with minimal maintenance
- Planning permission NOT needed for most domestic installations
- Households typically export 10-40% of generation, earning €150-€400/year

## INSULATION — THE FIRST PRIORITY
Insulation should ALWAYS be the first upgrade. No point installing a heat pump in a poorly insulated home.

### Priority Order
1. Attic insulation (cheapest, biggest impact per euro)
2. Cavity wall insulation (if applicable)
3. Draught-proofing (windows, doors, letterbox, chimney)
4. Hot water cylinder lagging jacket (€20-€40, DIY)
5. External/internal wall insulation (expensive but transformative for solid-wall homes)
6. Window and door upgrades (new grants from March 2026)

### DIY Quick Wins (No Grant Needed)
- Draught-proof doors and windows with foam strips (€10-€20)
- Fit a lagging jacket on hot water cylinder (€20-€40, saves ~€100/year)
- Close curtains at dusk to retain heat
- Use reflective radiator panels behind radiators on external walls (€15-€30)
- Bleed radiators regularly for efficient heating
- Set heating thermostat to 20°C (each degree above costs ~10% more)
- Use a timer — don't leave heating on all day
- Close doors to unused rooms
- LED bulbs throughout (save ~€50-€100/year vs old bulbs)
- Seal gaps around pipes, cables, and floorboards with expandable foam

## BEHAVIOURAL SAVINGS (No Cost)
These changes cost nothing and can save over €1,232/year combined:

Space heating (61% of home energy):
- Reduce thermostat by 1°C: saves ~10% on heating = ~€164/year (~€7/week)
- Cut heating by 1 hour/day: saves ~€234/year (~€9.38/week)
- Heat only occupied rooms with portable heater instead of central heating: saves ~€6/week

Hot water (20% of home energy):
- Shorten electric shower by 2-4 minutes: saves €109/year (shower costs €3.95/hour at 9.5kW)
- Lag hot water cylinder with 80mm jacket: reduces water heating cost by 30%

Appliances:
- Stop using tumble dryer (air-dry instead): saves €176/year (€3.40/week)
- Run dishwasher 3x/week instead of 6x: saves €87/year
- Only boil water you need in kettle (2.5kW = €1.03/hour)
- Unplug devices on standby: saves €50-€80/year
- Wash clothes at 30°C instead of 40°C
- Use lids on pots when cooking
- Keep fridge at 3-5°C, freezer at -18°C (not colder)
- LED bulbs throughout: save ~€50-€100/year vs old bulbs
